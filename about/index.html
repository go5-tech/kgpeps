What Are Research Peptides?

Peptides are short chains of amino acids — the same building blocks that make up all proteins in the human body. While proteins can contain hundreds of amino acids, peptides typically contain 2 to 50, making them small enough to be synthesised in a laboratory with precise molecular control.

Research peptides are synthetically produced peptide compounds manufactured for use in in-vitro scientific studies. They allow researchers to study specific biological pathways, cellular mechanisms, and physiological processes in a controlled laboratory environment. All products supplied by Peptide Central are for in-vitro research use only and are not approved for human consumption.
