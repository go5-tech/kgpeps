Buy AOD-9604 in India: HGH Fragment Research Guide

Published 2026-06-01 · Peptide Central Research Team · 7 min read

AOD-9604 is a synthetic peptide corresponding to amino acids 177–191 at the C-terminus of human growth hormone (HGH). It is often called "HGH Fragment 177–191" and represents the region of the HGH molecule that researchers identified as responsible for its fat-metabolising activity — isolated and stabilised as a standalone compound.

What Is AOD-9604?

The full HGH molecule performs multiple physiological roles: stimulating IGF-1 production, promoting lean muscle accrual, influencing glucose metabolism, and triggering lipolysis (fat breakdown). Researchers at Monash University in Australia set out to identify which portion of the HGH sequence was responsible for lipid regulation. The answer was the C-terminal fragment spanning residues 177 to 191 — now known as AOD-9604.

The "AOD" designation stands for "Anti-Obesity Drug," reflecting the compound's original research trajectory. The peptide contains a tyrosine residue at its N-terminus that is not present in native HGH, which was introduced to stabilise the molecule and improve its activity profile.

Mechanism: Lipolysis Without IGF-1 Elevation

The critical distinction in AOD-9604 research is the separation of lipolytic activity from growth-promoting activity. Full-length HGH elevates IGF-1 systemically, which drives cell proliferation and anabolic effects throughout the body. This comes with well-documented downstream consequences when used outside physiological ranges.

AOD-9604 does not bind to the GH receptor in a manner that triggers IGF-1 production. Preclinical studies show it activates fat breakdown through a mechanism involving beta-3 adrenergic receptors and direct effects on adipocyte metabolism — stimulating lipolysis (release of stored triglycerides) and inhibiting lipogenesis (new fat formation from carbohydrates) — without measurable IGF-1 elevation. This makes it a focused tool for adipose tissue research.

Additionally, AOD-9604 has demonstrated cartilage and bone regeneration activity in preclinical studies, suggesting a secondary research application beyond body composition.

Research Dosage Protocol

Standard research dosage: 300 mcg per day, administered subcutaneously

Timing: Morning administration on an empty stomach is commonly used in protocols, as elevated insulin can blunt lipolytic signalling

Half-life: Approximately 30–60 minutes; some protocols split into two 150 mcg injections (morning and pre-sleep)

Cycle length: Most research protocols run 12–16 weeks to assess effects on adipose tissue

AOD-9604 vs MOTS-C for Metabolic Research

Researchers studying metabolic pathways often compare AOD-9604 with MOTS-C, the mitochondria-derived peptide. These two compounds target fat metabolism through entirely different mechanisms: AOD-9604 acts primarily at the adipocyte level via adrenergic pathways, while MOTS-C operates upstream in mitochondrial metabolism, improving insulin sensitivity and AMPK activation. Some researchers stack both for complementary coverage of lipolytic and metabolic-efficiency pathways, though they represent distinct research vectors.
