What to Look For in a Peptide Vendor

1. Independent HPLC Verification

The most important trust signal is third-party HPLC (High-Performance Liquid Chromatography) testing. Any reputable vendor will test every batch at an independent lab and provide results. If a vendor only claims purity without providing lab data, avoid them.

2. Certificate of Analysis (COA)

A COA should accompany every single order — not just be available on request. It confirms the identity, purity, and absence of contaminants for that specific batch.

3. Professional Contact Information

A vendor with a professional domain email (e.g. info@peptidecentral.in) signals legitimacy. Be cautious of vendors using generic Gmail addresses as their primary contact.

4. COD & Transparent Pricing

Cash on Delivery availability is a major trust signal — it means the vendor is confident enough in their product to ship before payment. Look for transparent, consistent pricing without excessive "limited time" pressure tactics.

Red Flags to Avoid

  • No COA available or COA only on request
  • No mention of HPLC testing methodology
  • Extremely low prices (99.9% purity at ₹500/vial is not real)
  • No WhatsApp or direct support channel
  • No return or refund policy
