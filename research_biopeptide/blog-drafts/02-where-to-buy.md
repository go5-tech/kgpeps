## Red Flags: What NOT to Buy From

### No Lab Reports at All

If a vendor has zero HPLC data, chromatograms, or testing documentation, assume the product is untested, underdosed, or contaminated. Don't buy.

### Suspiciously Cheap Prices

Peptide research is expensive. Legitimate HPLC testing costs money. If a vendor's prices are 40-50% below market average with no explanation, the product quality is likely compromised.

**Realistic pricing rule:** If it seems too cheap, it's probably fake or severely underdosed.

### No COD, Advance Payment Only

Requiring 100% prepayment with no return option is a scam setup. Legitimate vendors accept COD because they're confident in their product.

### Cannot Provide Batch-Specific CoA

Reputable vendors track batches and can provide CoA for the specific batch you're buying, not a generic "typical analysis" document.

Ask your vendor: "Can you provide the CoA for batch #XYZ before I order?"

If they hesitate or provide a generic report, they're cutting corners.

### No Address, Only Email or Social Media

Legitimate businesses register addresses. If a vendor has only an email and a WhatsApp number with no official address or GST registration details, it's a red flag.

### Inconsistent Product Information

If their website says "95% purity" but marketing materials claim "99.9%," something's wrong. Consistency matters.
