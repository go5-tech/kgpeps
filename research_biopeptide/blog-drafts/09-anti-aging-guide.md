## Understanding Aging: The Cellular Perspective

Before discussing anti-aging peptides, it's important to understand what researchers mean by "aging."

**Hallmarks of aging** (as defined by modern geroscience):
1. **Genomic instability** – DNA damage accumulation
2. **Telomere attrition** – Shortening of protective chromosome caps
3. **Epigenetic alterations** – Changes in gene expression
4. **Loss of proteostasis** – Protein folding dysfunction
5. **Mitochondrial dysfunction** – Energy production decline
6. **Cellular senescence** – Permanent cell cycle arrest
7. **Stem cell exhaustion** – Reduced regenerative capacity
8. **Altered intercellular communication** – Inflammatory signaling

Anti-aging peptides target one or more of these hallmarks. The research goal is to slow, arrest, or reverse these processes at the cellular level.
