## The 10-Point Vendor Evaluation Checklist

### 1. HPLC Purity Verification (99%+ Published)

**Why it matters:** HPLC (High-Performance Liquid Chromatography) is the gold standard for peptide testing. A vendor claiming 99% purity without HPLC data is guessing.

**The check:**
- [ ] Vendor publishes HPLC purity data on their website or CoA
- [ ] Purity is stated as a percentage (e.g., "99.7%")
- [ ] Data includes specific details (retention time, gradient, methodology)
- [ ] Purity is 95%+ at minimum (98%+ is better)

**Red flag:** Vendor says "high purity" without a number or test data.

**Example:** Peptide Central publishes 99.9% HPLC purity for all products. This sets the gold standard.

---

### 2. Certificate of Analysis (CoA) With Every Order

**Why it matters:** A CoA is a lab document proving the specific batch you're buying has been tested. It's the only objective proof of quality.

**The check:**
- [ ] Vendor provides batch-specific CoA (not generic)
- [ ] CoA is available before you order (not after)
- [ ] CoA includes HPLC chromatogram or chart
- [ ] CoA lists the testing lab with contact info
- [ ] CoA shows purity percentage, water content, and identity confirmation

**Red flag:** Vendor says "CoA available upon request" (delay tactic) or provides same CoA for all customers (forged).

**How to verify:** Call the lab listed on the CoA. Ask: "Did you test batch #[batch number] on [date]?" A real lab will confirm.

### 3. COD Availability (Pan-India)

**Why it matters:** Cash on Delivery means you receive the product, inspect it, and verify the CoA before paying. This eliminates payment fraud risk.

**The check:**
- [ ] Vendor offers COD across India (or at least in major cities)
- [ ] COD is the default option (not premium add-on)
- [ ] No "prepayment required" disclaimer
- [ ] Delivery time estimate is clear (e.g., "3–7 days")

**Red flag:** Vendor requires 100% prepayment or only accepts international payment (Western Union, Crypto).

**Why Peptide Central's COD:** Pan-India COD in 3–7 days is a trust signal. It means they're confident enough to deliver first, get paid later.

### 4. Purity Percentage Listed (Not Just "Verified")

**Why it matters:** "Verified purity" is vague. "99.7% purity via HPLC" is concrete and comparable.

**The check:**
- [ ] Purity is stated as a specific percentage (99.2%, 98.5%, etc.)
- [ ] Not just "verified" or "high purity"
- [ ] Percentage is consistent across multiple products or batches
- [ ] Purity meets or exceeds 95% (preferably 98%+)

**Realistic pricing rule:**
- 95–96% purity: ₹1,500–₹2,000 per 10mg
- 98–99% purity: ₹2,000–₹3,000 per 10mg
- 99.5%+ purity: ₹2,500–₹3,500 per 10mg

If someone's offering 99%+ purity at 50% below these prices, the purity claim is false.

### 5. Testing Method Disclosed (HPLC, LC-MS, or MALDI-TOF)

**Why it matters:** Different testing methods verify different aspects. HPLC is standard; MS adds confirmation.

**The check:**
- [ ] Vendor specifies the testing method (HPLC, LC-MS, MALDI-TOF, etc.)
- [ ] Testing method is appropriate for the peptide
- [ ] Not just "lab tested" without methodology

**Common methods:**
- **HPLC** – Purity via peak separation (universal standard)
- **LC-MS** – Purity + molecular weight confirmation (better)
- **MALDI-TOF** – Molecular weight + structure verification (best)

HPLC alone is acceptable. HPLC + MS is excellent.

### 6. India-Based Operations or Transparent Import Source

**Why it matters:** Knowing where a vendor sources helps you understand supply chain reliability and regulatory compliance.

**The check:**
- [ ] Vendor is India-based with registered address (GST verifiable)
- [ ] OR vendor transparently states import source (e.g., "HPLC-verified from [lab name], imported under [regulations]")
- [ ] Vendor has a physical address (not just WhatsApp)
- [ ] Company is registered (searchable on GST India portal)

**How to verify:**
- Search vendor name + "GST" on https://www.gst.gov.in/
- Look up address on Google Maps
- Call and confirm the address matches their website

**Red flag:** Vendor has no address or only a WhatsApp number.

### 9. Batch Traceability (Batch Numbers Match CoA)

**Why it matters:** A real vendor tracks every batch. If you can't match your order to a specific batch number and CoA, the vendor is cutting corners.

**The check:**
- [ ] Vendor provides batch number before or at purchase
- [ ] Batch number on the powder vial matches the CoA batch
- [ ] Batch number is on the invoice
- [ ] Vendor can retrieve CoA by batch number

**How to verify:** Before accepting delivery, ask: "What's the batch number of the peptide I'm receiving?" If they don't know, something's wrong.

---

### 10. Professional Packaging and Insulation

**Why it matters:** Peptides are damaged by heat, humidity, and light. Professional vendors protect their products.

**The check:**
- [ ] Packaging is professional and sealed (not taped hastily)
- [ ] Insulated box with ice packs for transit (important in India's heat)
- [ ] Vials are in sealed, labeled containers
- [ ] Desiccant packets included
- [ ] Instructions for storage included

**Upon arrival:**
- Package should feel cool (ice packs worked)
- Vial should be sealed and intact
- Label should match your order

**Red flag:** Package arrives warm, vials are loose, or no insulation was used.
