## The Retatrutide Mechanism: Triple Synergy

### GLP-1 Action: Satiety and Glucose Control

GLP-1 signaling suppresses appetite through direct brain signaling (vagal) and gastric signaling. It delays gastric emptying, increasing meal satiety. Simultaneously, it stimulates insulin secretion in a glucose-dependent manner—only when blood glucose is elevated.

This mechanism explains early satiety and reduced food intake without hypoglycemia risk.

### GIP Action: Metabolic Rate and Energy Expenditure

GIP activation increases resting metabolic rate and energy expenditure. Unlike GLP-1, which primarily suppresses intake, GIP increases calorie expenditure. This dual mechanism (reduced intake + increased expenditure) drives weight loss.

GIP also improves insulin secretion postprandially, supporting glucose control.

### Glucagon Action: Fat Mobilization and Lipolysis

Glucagon activates hepatic and adipose tissue lipolysis—fat breakdown. It elevates metabolic rate and increases thermogenesis. Glucagon typically raises glucose (problematic in diabetes), but in combination with GLP-1's insulin secretion, the net effect is controlled.

Retatrutide's glucagon component is the innovation—previous compounds avoided glucagon due to hyperglycemia risk. Retatrutide's design balances this.

### Synergistic Effects: Why Triple Beats Dual

Individual mechanisms are potent. Triple combination creates:
- **Maximal appetite suppression:** GLP-1 central + GIP metabolic effects
- **Maximal calorie expenditure:** GIP metabolic rate + glucagon thermogenesis
- **Maximal fat mobilization:** Glucagon lipolysis + reduced lipogenesis
- **Maintained glucose control:** GLP-1 insulin support counterbalances glucagon

The result: greater weight loss with maintained metabolic health.
