## The Epitalon Mechanism: Telomerase and Cellular Aging

### Telomerase Activation and Telomere Extension

Epitalon's primary mechanism is telomerase activation. Telomeres are DNA caps on chromosome ends that shorten with each cell division. When telomeres reach critical length, cells either senesce (stop dividing) or die. Telomere length is a fundamental aging clock.

Telomerase is the enzyme that rebuilds telomeres, but it's suppressed in most human somatic cells (except immune cells, bone marrow, skin). Epitalon reactivates telomerase expression, allowing telomere extension.

This is longevity research's holy grail: extending the cellular division limit extends organism lifespan and delays age-related disease onset.
