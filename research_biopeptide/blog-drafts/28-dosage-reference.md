## BPC-157 (Body Protection Compound-157)

**Category:** Recovery & Healing Peptide

**Typical Dose Range:** 250–500 mcg per injection

**Frequency:** Once daily (some protocols use 2x daily)

**Half-life:** ~6 hours

**Best Timing:** Morning or post-injury (for localized healing, inject near injury site)

**Vial assumption:** 5mg = 5000 mcg total

**Protocol example:**
- 250 mcg subq daily for 8-12 weeks
- A 5mg vial provides 20 daily doses (20 days supply)
- Cost per dose: ₹175–250

**Timeline to effects:**
- Week 1-2: Minimal acute effects
- Week 2-4: Recovery improvement, reduced soreness
- Week 4-8: Significant healing acceleration (if injury-focused protocol)
- Week 8+: Cumulative tissue repair, sustained benefits

**Notes:**
- Highly effective for localized injuries; inject near injury site when possible
- Can be used systemically (subq abdomen) for gut/general healing
- Stacks well with TB-500 for comprehensive recovery
- No known contraindications

**Storage:** Reconstitute in 2-2.5mL bacteriostatic water; refrigerate 2-4 weeks

## Common Dosing Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Using too high starting doses** – Always start at lower end of range; increase if needed
2. **Inconsistent timing** – Peptide efficacy depends on consistent dosing schedules
3. **Skipping doses** – Missing injections disrupts consistency; maintain protocol discipline
4. **Not accounting for half-life** – Shorter half-life peptides need more frequent dosing for sustained effect
5. **Mixing vial batches** – Reconstitute one vial completely before opening another (prevents contamination)
6. **Measuring inaccurately** – Use syringe markings precisely; poor measurement = inconsistent dosing
7. **Underdosing out of caution** – Lower doses mean slower/weaker results; follow range guidelines
8. **Not rotating injection sites** – Same spot repeatedly causes tissue damage; rotate sites
9. **Failing to refrigerate reconstituted peptides** – Degradation happens quickly at room temp
10. **Reusing needles** – Infection risk; always use fresh needles

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I do higher doses than listed?**
A: Generally no. Higher doses don't proportionally increase effects and risk side effects. Follow established ranges.

**Q: What if I miss a dose?**
A: Take the next dose at normal time. Don't double-dose to compensate; it disrupts the protocol.

**Q: Should I cycle off peptides or use continuously?**
A: Most peptides work indefinitely without tolerance. Cycling is optional (e.g., 8 weeks on, 2 weeks off). Neither is required.

**Q: Can I combine peptides not listed here?**
A: Yes, many stacks exist. Start with established stacks (like GH stack: CJC + Ipamorelin). Consult Peptide Central support for custom stacks.

**Q: How do I measure ultra-small doses (5-10 mcg)?**
A: Insulin syringes (30-unit) allow precise measurement. Use calculator: (dose needed ÷ concentration per mL) × 100 = units on syringe.

**Q: Does timing of day matter for all peptides?**
A: Some (stimulants/secretagogues): yes, morning/midday. Others (recovery): timing is flexible. Consistency matters more than specific time.

**Q: What if I accidentally inject double the dose?**
A: Most peptides are safe at double doses (side effects increase, but toxicity is rare). Monitor for side effects; inform support if concerned.
