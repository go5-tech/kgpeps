## What is HPLC Testing?

**HPLC (High-Performance Liquid Chromatography)** is the gold-standard analytical technique for measuring peptide purity and verifying identity. Here's how it works:

### The HPLC Process

**1. Sample Preparation**  
Your lyophilized peptide is dissolved in a specific solvent (usually water or dilute acid) to create a solution for analysis.

**2. Injection**  
A tiny, precise volume of the sample solution is injected into the HPLC system through an autosampler.

**3. Column Separation**  
The liquid sample travels through a specialized analytical column (typically reverse-phase C18) under high pressure (up to 300 bar). The column is packed with microscopic particles that interact with the peptide and any impurities differently.

**4. Differential Retention**  
Molecules are separated based on their chemical properties (hydrophobicity, charge, size). The intended peptide and impurities travel through the column at different speeds, creating physical separation.

**5. Detection & Measurement**  
As each compound exits the column, it passes through a UV detector (typically at 214 nm or 254 nm wavelength). The detector measures the light absorbed by each component.

**6. Chromatogram Generation**  
The detector output creates a chromatogram—a graph showing peaks for each compound, with peak area proportional to concentration.

**7. Purity Calculation**  
Software calculates purity by dividing the area of the target peptide peak by the total area of all peaks (target + impurities). Purity is expressed as a percentage.

### Why HPLC?

- **Sensitivity:** Detects impurities at 0.01% concentration or lower
- **Accuracy:** Provides precise, quantifiable purity data
- **Reliability:** Reproducible results that meet international standards (USP, EP, BP)
- **Industry Standard:** Accepted by regulatory bodies, journals, and institutions worldwide
- **Non-destructive:** Doesn't destroy the sample, though small amounts are used

## What is Mass Spectrometry (MS) Verification?

**Mass Spectrometry (MS)** is a complementary technique used to verify peptide **identity**—confirming that you have the exact peptide you ordered, not a structurally similar impurity or mistake.

### How MS Works

**1. Ionization**  
The peptide sample is converted into charged ions using ionization methods (ESI, MALDI, or others).

**2. Mass Separation**  
Ions are accelerated through an electric field and separated based on their mass-to-charge ratio (m/z).

**3. Detection**  
A detector records the m/z of each ion, generating a mass spectrum.

**4. Fingerprint Comparison**  
The resulting spectrum is compared to the known theoretical mass of the target peptide. If it matches, identity is confirmed.

**5. Fragmentation Analysis (optional)**  
For additional certainty, the peptide can be fragmented further (tandem MS / MS/MS) to confirm sequence and structure.

### Why Both HPLC and MS?

- **HPLC** answers: "How pure is my peptide?" (quantity of target vs. impurities)
- **MS** answers: "Is this actually the peptide I ordered?" (confirmation of molecular identity)

Together, they provide complete quality assurance. HPLC ensures purity; MS ensures identity. Peptide Central uses both techniques for premium verification.

## How to Read a Certificate of Analysis

Your Certificate of Analysis contains several key sections. Here's how to interpret them:

### 1. Header Information
- **Laboratory name and credentials** (accreditation, ISO status)
- **COA issue date** and expiry date
- **Batch number** (links to manufacturing date and conditions)
- **Product name** (e.g., "BPC-157")

### 2. Sample Identification
- **Product description** (e.g., "BPC-157, lyophilized powder")
- **Batch/lot number** (unique identifier for your specific batch)
- **Quantity received** and **quantity tested**
- **Date received** and **date analyzed**

### 3. Purity Analysis
- **HPLC purity result** (e.g., "99.8% by HPLC")
- **Method reference** (USP, EP, or custom method)
- **Solvent system** used
- **Retention time** of the target peptide
- **Peak area percentage** of the target compound

### 4. Identity Confirmation
- **Mass spectrometry result** (if performed)
- **Theoretical mass** vs. **observed mass**
- **Calculated molecular weight**
- **Match status** (confirmed, consistent, or flag for review)

### 5. Other Quality Parameters
- **Moisture content** (typically <5% for lyophilized peptides)
- **Water content** (Karl Fischer titration)
- **Appearance** (color, form—should be white to off-white powder)
- **Solubility** in specified solvents (if tested)

### 6. Safety & Compliance Testing
- **Sterility testing** (bacterial and fungal culture)
- **Endotoxin levels** (LAL test, if applicable)
- **Heavy metals** screening (lead, mercury, cadmium)
- **Microbial limits** (if required)

### 7. Storage & Stability
- **Recommended storage conditions** (temperature, light, container)
- **Shelf life** (expiry date)
- **Stability data** (if available)

### 8. Signature & Approval
- **Analyst signature** (who performed the testing)
- **QA approval** (quality assurance verification)
- **Laboratory stamp/seal** (official validation)

### How to Verify Authenticity

Check:
1. **Laboratory credentials** — Is the lab accredited? (Look for ISO 17025 or equivalent)
2. **Signature & seal** — Are they present and legible?
3. **Batch number** — Does it match your vial label?
4. **Purity result** — Is it 99.0% or higher? (99.9% is our standard)
5. **Date** — Is it recent (within 3 months of receipt)?
