What is Selank?

Selank is a synthetic heptapeptide analogue of the immunomodulatory peptide tuftsin, developed by the Institute of Molecular Genetics of the Russian Academy of Sciences. With the sequence Thr-Lys-Pro-Arg-Pro-Gly-Pro, it modulates GABA-A receptors, enhances serotonin and enkephalin activity, and reduces cortisol — producing anxiolytic and nootropic effects without the sedation, dependence, or withdrawal associated with traditional anxiolytics like benzodiazepines.

Research Benefits

  • Modulates GABA-A receptors to produce anxiolytic effects without sedation or dependence
  • Reduces cortisol and markers of physiological stress response
  • Enhances serotonin metabolism — researched for mood stabilization and depression models
  • Improves memory consolidation and learning through enkephalin system activation
  • Immunomodulatory effects — derived from tuftsin, a natural immune-stimulating peptide
  • Anti-anxiety effects without cognitive impairment — "clear-headed calm" in research models

Dosage & Protocol

Research protocols use 250–500 mcg per day administered intranasally or subcutaneously. The ~8-hour half-life supports once or twice-daily administration. Intranasal delivery of 150–250 mcg per nostril is the most studied route for CNS targeting. Research cycles of 2–4 weeks are standard, with reassessment of effects.
