What is Semax?

Semax is a synthetic heptapeptide analogue of the N-terminal fragment of ACTH (adrenocorticotropic hormone), developed in Russia and approved for medical use in the Russian Federation for cognitive impairment and ischemic conditions. Its sequence (Met-Glu-His-Phe-Pro-Gly-Pro) upregulates Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor (BDNF), promotes myelination, and enhances dopaminergic and serotonergic neurotransmission — making it one of the most well-researched peptide nootropics available.

Research Benefits

  • Upregulates BDNF (Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor) — critical for neuroplasticity and learning
  • Enhances working memory, focus, and information processing speed
  • Promotes myelination — the protective coating of nerve fibers essential for neural signal speed
  • Studied for neuroprotection following ischemic injury and cognitive decline
  • Modulates dopaminergic and serotonergic systems for improved mood and motivation
  • Anti-anxiety effects via enkephalin system modulation without sedation

Dosage & Protocol

Research protocols use 300–600 mcg per day administered intranasally or subcutaneously. Intranasal delivery is preferred for direct CNS access via the olfactory epithelium. The ~8-hour half-life supports once or twice-daily dosing. Standard research cycles are 2–4 weeks with sensitivity reassessment periods.
